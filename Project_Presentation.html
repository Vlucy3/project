Project Overview

  • Objective: Predict daily sales for 54 supermarkets in Ecuador across 33 product families.
  • Dataset: Kaggle Store Sales – Time Series Forecasting.
  • The Challenge: Capture complex seasonality, holiday impacts, and external economic factors (Oil Prices).

Methodology: Step-by-Step

  1. Data Preparation: Filtered data from 2016+ to focus on modern patterns and clipped 99.9th percentile outliers.
  2. Feature Engineering: Merged stores, oil prices, and transactions. Created "Lagged Transactions" as a foot-traffic proxy.
  3. Temporal Engineering: Added cyclical features (Sines/Cosines), Payday signals, and complex Lags (16 to 35 days).
  4. Target Encoding: Calculated historical means per Store-Family to capture baseline performance.

Key Business Insights

  • Promotions Work: "On Promotion" status is the single strongest predictor of sales spikes.
  • The Payday Effect: Sales consistently peak around the 15th and 30th of each month.
  • Regional Nuance: Local holidays have a distinct impact compared to National holidays, requiring granular mapping.
  • Lag Dependency: The 16-day and 28-day lags are critical, capturing both the immediate past and the monthly cycle.

Future Improvements

  • Hyperparameter Optimization: Use Bayesian optimization (Optuna) to fine-tune tree depth and learning rates.
  • Deep Learning Hybrid: Incorporate the Transformer model's attention mechanism to better handle long-range dependencies.
  • Stacking: Instead of simple weighting, use a "Meta-Learner" (like a Ridge Regression) to combine model outputs.
  • External Data: Add weather data or local competitor pricing if available.
